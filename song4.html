4. Mohobbat Karne Wale

मोहब्बत करने वाले कम न होंगे

This ghazal by Hafeez Jalandhari explores themes of unfulfilled love, longing, and separation. The poet reflects on the idea that while there may be many admirers of the beloved, he himself will not be among them. He contrasts the weight of personal sorrow against the world's collective grief, suggesting that the pain of loving and losing the beloved surpasses all other troubles. Despite the possibility of reunion, the scars of separation remain, making love a bittersweet experience. The poem beautifully captures the essence of heartbreak, quiet acceptance, and the melancholic fate of the unrequited lover.

Lyrics

मोहब्बत करने वाले कम न होंगे तिरी महफ़िल में लेकिन हम न होंगे मैं अक्सर सोचता हूँ फूल कब तक शरीक-ए-गिर्या-ए-शबनम न होंगे ज़रा देर-आश्ना चश्म-ए-करम है सितम ही इश्क़ में पैहम न होंगे दिलों की उलझनें बढ़ती रहेंगी अगर कुछ मशवरे बाहम न होंगे ज़माने भर के ग़म या इक तिरा ग़म ये ग़म होगा तो कितने ग़म न होंगे कहूँ बेदर्द क्यूँ अहल-ए-जहाँ को वो मेरे हाल से महरम न होंगे हमारे दिल में सैल-ए-गिर्या होगा अगर बा-दीदा-ए-पुर-नम न होंगे अगर तू इत्तिफ़ाक़न मिल भी जाए तिरी फ़ुर्क़त के सदमे कम न होंगे 'हफ़ीज़' उन से मैं जितना बद-गुमाँ हूँ वो मुझ से उस क़दर बरहम न होंगे

Translation

There will be no shortage of those who love you, But in your gathering, I will not be present. I often wonder, how long will flowers Continue to share the tears of the dewdrop? Your merciful gaze is fleeting, Yet love is not always filled with suffering. The confusions of hearts will only grow, If lovers do not share their counsel. The grief of the entire world or just your sorrow— If your pain exists, what other pains could remain? Why should I call the people of the world heartless? They are simply unaware of my condition. My heart will be flooded with tears, If my eyes do not carry their usual sorrow. Even if by some chance I meet you, The pain of separation will never diminish. Hafiz, no matter how much I distrust them, They will never be as displeased with me as I am with them.
